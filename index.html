Types of Guns

Education

Eddie Eagle

The Eddie Eagle GunSafe® program is a gun accident prevention program that seeks to help parents, law enforcement, community groups and educators navigate a topic paramount to our children’s safety. Eddie and his Wing Team are on a mission to help you teach Pre-K through 4th graders what to do if they ever come across a gun… “Stop. Don’t Touch. Run Away. Tell a Grown-Up.”

Hunter Education

NRA Hunter Education’s award-winning and 100% free online course is designed to help new hunters of all ages learn how to be safe and responsible members of the hunting community. From the organization that built the first-ever hunter education program in 1949, this state-of-the-art course is the most comprehensive online hunter education instruction in the United States. The 15-chapter, online sequence featuring attention-grabbing videos, eye-catching graphics, interactive modules, audio recordings and dozens of action photos is available at students’ fingertips, whenever and wherever they are able to complete it.

NRA Law Enforcement Division

Law Enforcement, Military and Security. The NRA established a Law Enforcement Division in 1960 specifically to provide the law enforcement community with a means to certify law enforcement firearm instructors. We also offer law enforcement firearm competitions, as we believe competition is a direct extension of training. Competitions consist of the National Police Shooting Championships as well as a new multi-gun law enforcement competition called the Tactical Police Competition, or TPC.

Youth Education Summit (Y.E.S)

For two decades, NRA’s Youth Education Summit (Y.E.S.) has brought of some of America’s most bright, ambitious, and patriotic youth to our nation's capital each summer for a week of fun they'll never forget. Students engage with political leaders, visit historical monuments, and apply their skills in competitive debates, all to enrich and inspire the future of our nation. The mission of the Y.E.S. program is to help students explore our American government, Bill of Rights and the U.S. Constitution, while encouraging them to become responsible citizens who participate in the democratic process and return to their community with a proactive voice for the Second Amendment.

Refuse to be a Victim

Basic principles for crime prevention and personal safety. We never think a crime can happen to us, until it does. While sometimes unfortunate things do happen unexpectedly, it is important to be equipped with the knowledge and skills for personal safety and crime prevention.

Gun Safety

The 3 cardinal rules of gun safety are:

  • Always keep the gun pointed in a safe direction.
  • Always keep your finger off the trigger until you are ready to shoot.
  • Always keep the gun unloaded until ready to use.

School Shield

We Have A Singular Mission: To Protect Our Children

School security is a complex issue with no simple, single solution. The NRA School Shield program is committed to addressing the many facets of school security, including best practices in security infrastructure, technology, personnel, training, and policy. Through this multidimensional effort, NRA School Shield seeks to engage communities and empower leaders to help make our schools more secure.

Youth Hunter Education (YHEC)

NRA's Youth Hunter Education Challenge (YHEC) provides a fun environment for kids to improve their hunting marksmanship, and safety skills. Through its simulated hunting situations, live fire exercises, educational and responsibility events, YHEC helps build upon skills learned in basic hunter education courses and encourages safer, lifelong hunting habits. From rifle, bow, and muzzleloader shooting at life-sized targets, to wildlife identification, map and compass orienteering, and more, YHEC participants can get hands-on training in eight skill areas, giving them expertise in all methods of take and all types of game.

Who can legally own or posses a gun under federal law?
  • U.S. Citizens
  • U.S. Nationals who are not Citizens
  • Lawful Permanant Residents ("green card" holders)
  • Certain Non-Citizens (e.g., refugees, some nonimmigrant visa holders who meet specific exceptions like sporting or hunting purposes)

How to Hunt in Texas?
  • Get a hunting license - Required for all hunters (residents and non-residents). Buy online at TPWD website or from licensed vendors
  • Complete Hunter Education - Mandatory for anyone born on/after Sept. 2, 1971. The course is available fully online with a printable temporary certificate.
  • Obtain Required Permits - APH Permit for public lands; apply for draw hunts where required. Always check unit-specific permit rules.
  • Find a Plane to Hunt - Public land through the APH program or private land (with permision/lease). Over 95% of Texas is private property.
  • Know the Rules - Use the TPWD Outdoor Annual and APH booklet for regulations on seasons, legal game, and area-specific requirements.
Is it safe to have guns in the home?

Gun owner ship can become risky instead of protective if the proper steps aren't taken. Having guns in house can be safe if:

  • They are stored securely.
  • Everyone with access is trained and responsible.
  • Children are taught safety rules.
  • The household has a clear plan for both security and emergencies.
